## 2. CORE FRAMEWORKS

### **Learning as Behavior Change**
- **Definition:** If after consuming content you return to the same conditions and change nothing, you learned nothing. Learning is functionally only entertainment if behavior doesn't change.
- **Timestamp:** 4:17-4:47
- **Key Quote:** "The difference between people who are entertained by business content and people who do business is that they actually change your behavior."
- **Strongest Definition:** "Same conditions, new behavior—you turn off the computer, you look back, you're at home, you're at your workplace, whatever. If nothing changes, you learned nothing."

### **Skills as Chained Adaptive Behaviors**
- **Definition:** Skills are not monolithic but are chains of multiple adaptive behaviors strung together. Business success requires breaking down chunked concepts into smallest behavioral components.
- **Timestamp:** 5:15-6:48
- **Example Given:** Basketball isn't "being good at basketball"—it's good at dribbling, shooting, passing; within those are free throws, three-pointers, jump shots, hooks, etc.
- **Strongest Definition:** "Skills are actually a chain of adaptive behaviors. If you chain multiple behaviors together, then you get a skill. Skills are chunked."

### **Constraints, Leverage, and Compounding**
- **Definition:** Every business has a constraint. To grow, you must identify and deconstrain it through leverage. When done successfully, this creates compounding growth.
- **Timestamp:** 3:44-4:01
- **Application:** "Every business has a constraint. We need to deconstrain it. In order to deconstrain it more effectively, we use leverage. If we do that successfully from a model perspective, we achieve compounding."
- **Strongest Definition:** "Every business has a constraint. Uh we need to deconstrain it. In order to deconstra it more effectively, we use leverage. If we do that uh successfully from a model perspective, we achieve compounding. If we do those three things, we have a business that will continue to grow."

### **The Attitude/Aptitude Framework (Hiring)**
- **Definition:** The key hiring principle is to hire for the smallest skill deficiency. In low-skilled roles, hire for attitude; in high-skilled specialized roles, hire for aptitude (skill).
- **Timestamp:** 11:51-14:15
- **Application:** "If I have higher one and higher two, right? If I have something that the aptitude that I'm trying to teach takes, you know, a day, I'm for sure gonna hire for attitude... This would take me years. So, I'm going to hire for the small skill deficiency."
- **Critical Insight:** The answer "depends on the nature of the role. But the the the single golden truth is that you hire for the smallest skill deficiency."

### **The Goal Hierarchy: Hill, Mountain, Everest**
- **Definition:** Different business goals require different paths and models. Not everyone needs to build a trillion-dollar company; smaller goals are achievable through your existing vehicle with full commitment.
- **Timestamp:** 38:21-39:32
- **Key Quote:** "If your goal is to uh is to get to a a trillion dollar company, right? Well, then you pretty much exclude if you want to get to Everest, you pretty much would have to just do a global technology product."
- **Strongest Definition:** "Unless your goal is the trillion, then almost all of these things that I'm about to share with you will work in your existing vehicle."

### **Multiple Paths Up the Mountain**
- **Definition:** For any constraint, there are multiple ways to solve it. You choose the path where you have the highest likelihood of success given your existing resources and skills.
- **Timestamp:** 1:35-1:45 (conceptual intro), 1:39:29-1:40:02 (detailed application)
- **Example:** For leads that cost too much, you can improve ads, make better content, improve hooks, strengthen branding, adjust offers, add money model mechanisms, or refine avatar targeting.
- **Principle:** "We do the one that we believe we have the highest likelihood of succeeding with given our existing resources and skills."

### **The VSSL Framework (Video Sales System Letter)**
- **Definition:** A video that precedes any sales call to preload information your sales team would repeat. Dramatically shortens calls and improves conversions.
- **Timestamp:** 1:41:27-1:42:07
- **Strongest Definition:** "The function of a VSSL is to take all of the stuff that your sales team would repeat over and over again on a call and then just preload that with the customer so that when you talk to the customer, you can actually talk about them instead of the pitch."

### **The "Dangerous Middle" Concept**
- **Definition:** When revenue feels steady or you're working hard but not growing, you're likely actually demand-constrained, not supply-constrained. You need to break the equilibrium, generate cash flow, then expand capacity.
- **Timestamp:** 2:29:19-2:30:41
- **Key Quote:** "If you're in equilibrium, it's almost it's like I can't guarantee anything of course, but it's super likely that if you had to, you could do 30% more... sometimes you have to redline."

### **The Supply-Constrained Definition & Shift**
- **Definition:** Supply-constrained means you have demand but can't serve it due to people, talent, or capacity constraints. Most supply-constrained businesses are actually just demand-constrained for employees.
- **Timestamp:** 2:02:14-2:06:44
- **Key Insight:** "Since manpower means employees, it just means a different type of customer becomes the target. So if you are supply constrained, it means you're just demand constrained for employees."

### **LTGP to CAT Ratio** (Lifetime Gross Profit per employee to Cost of Acquiring Talent)
- **Definition:** The leveled-up version of LTV/CAC for supply-constrained businesses. What does it cost to hire talent, and what do you make from that talent?
- **Timestamp:** 2:06:49-2:07:15
- **Application:** "Instead of LTV to CAC ratios, you need to learn something that I call LTGP to CAT ratios, which is lifetime gross profit per employee to cost of acquiring talent."

### **The Entrepreneurial Cycle: Uninformed Optimism → Informed Pessimism → Valley of Despair → Informed Optimism → Achievement**
- **Definition:** The stages entrepreneurs go through, but many repeat stages 1-3 instead of pushing through to achievement because jumping to new ventures feels rewarding.
- **Timestamp:** 2:47:35-2:49:04
- **Problem:** "So many people just repeat one, you know, one, two, three over and over and over again. They get to the value to spare and then they say, you know what, there's this other thing that looks interesting."

### **CAC vs. LTGP Arbitrage for Avatars**
- **Definition:** Sometimes it's more valuable to target a customer that costs 5x more to acquire but is worth 20x more in lifetime value, rather than chasing cheaper customers with lower returns.
- **Timestamp:** 1:37:24-1:40:02
- **Principle:** "You have an avatar that costs you $500 to get that you make $10,000 from. You have a 20x and this is a 5x. So you have a 4x difference in relative return. The most valuable thing you can do is just decide to not sell to a specific customer base and then go up market to better avatars."

## 3. THE MOSY 6 / CONSTRAINT FRAMEWORK

The complete decision tree for identifying business constraints:

### **M - METRICS (Are your metrics clear?)**
- Do you know what your numbers are?
- Do you know how many leads come in?
- Do you know how many close?
- Do you know customer acquisition cost (CAC)?
- Do you know lifetime value (LTV)?
- **Timestamp:** 1:04:40-1:05:17
- **Action:** If metrics are unclear, this becomes priority #1. Stop growth pursuit, track basic business metrics before making capital decisions.
- **Key Quote:** "You can't afford leads because you don't know if more is work if any is working, right?"

### **O - OPPORTUNITY (Is the market big enough?)**
- Is there a 20x larger version of your business that already exists?
- Do you have a TAM (Total Addressable Market) problem or an execution problem?
- **Timestamp:** 1:29:26-1:29:39
- **Decision:** If opportunity exists, it's not the market; it's your mindset or execution.
- **Action:** Confirm TAM is sufficient; move to next filter.

### **S - SIZE (Will this model get you where you want?)**
- Does this business model actually work for your financial goals?
- Can you reach your revenue goal with the current business structure?
- Examples: One brick-and-mortar store can't reach $100M, but 20 can.
- **Timestamp:** 1:25:30-1:26:18
- **Action:** If model can't scale to goal, you need a different model. If it can, proceed.

### **Y - YOUR MINDSET (Are you all-in?)**
- Are you fully committed or "dating" the business (half in, half out)?
- Do you believe in the model long-term?
- **Timestamp:** 1:25:30-1:25:40
- **Problem:** Many owners waste years not going all-in, when full commitment would get them there faster.
- **Action:** Commit fully or change the model.

### **6 - THE MONEY: Four Sub-Categories**
Once you've confirmed metrics, market, model, and mindset are solid, the constraint is always in one of these four money categories:

#### **Option 1: LEADS (Cost to Acquire Customers is too high)**
- Your leads cost too much relative to what you make from them
- Check CAC: Is it industry average or above?
- **Solutions (Multiple Paths Up Mountain):**
  1. Better ads (Ad Playbook)
  2. Make better content (Hooks + Branding)
  3. Improve messaging (Branding)
  4. Better offer (Money Models - giveaway, win money back, decoy offer, pay less now/more later)
  5. Strategic avatar refinement (go after better-qualified people)
  6. CAC/LTV arbitrage (pay more for higher-value customers)

- **Decision Trees for Leads:**
  - If CAC is industry average AND you can't afford them → **LTV problem** (you make too little per customer)
  - If CAC is too high → Could be:
    - **Ads cost too much** (leads issue) → Better advertising, conversion optimization, lead magnets
    - **Close rate too low** (sales issue) → Sales process improvement
    - **Both** (multiplicative issue) → Work on weakest link first

#### **Option 2: SALES (Close Rate / Conversion is too low)**
- You get leads but can't convert them effectively
- **Solutions:**
  1. Conversion rate optimization on landing pages (headlines, sub-headlines, lead magnet)
  2. Better sales process (lead nurture, proof, VSSL)
  3. Follow-up systems (multiple touch points)
  4. Proof/social proof (testimonials, case studies)
  5. Training on closing technique
  6. Better interview process (for hiring leads)

- **Key Insight:** Most lead magnets default to "request invoice" or "book call" when they should offer more value (e.g., scaling roadmap attracts better leads than just a quote request).

#### **Option 3: LTV / DELIVERY (Make too little per customer)**
- You make money but not enough per customer to afford customer acquisition
- Your profit per customer is too low
- **Solutions:**
  1. Raise prices (with proper price increase letter)
  2. Increase customer lifespan (reduce churn)
  3. Increase customer volume (sell more to existing customers)
  4. Better avatar targeting (serve people who can pay more)
  5. Premium offerings (upsells, add-ons)
  6. Improve delivery to justify higher prices

#### **Option 4: CASH FLOW (Make enough but too slowly)**
- Profit exists but comes in too slowly relative to outflows
- Common in: Home services (net 90), software (months to recover CAC), recurring models
- **Solutions (9 tactics, in order of simplicity):**
  1. Push vendor terms from net 30 to net 60/90
  2. Payment terms restructuring:
     - Shift primary offer from month-to-month to quarterly (with annual discount)
     - Present high-to-low (annual price with interest, offer prepay discount)
     - Implement layaway (pay half upfront, start work, finish payment as you go)
  3. Prepay incentives (give people reason to pay in advance)
     - Guarantee with prepay (remove guarantee if not prepaid)
     - Most common reason prepay doesn't work: no incentive exists
  4. Upfront/onboarding fees
  5. Third-party BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) - typically 35% sales increase
  6. AR Financing (Accounts Receivable financing with banks for construction, known businesses)
  7. Multiple payment options

- **Key Story:** Insurance company stuck at $2-3M/year jumped to $5M/month just by getting vendor net-90 terms. Same leverage as AR financing, just from vendor side.

#### **Final Decision for Money Category:**
- **Start with CAC:** Is it industry average?
  - YES, average CAC but can't afford → **LTV is the problem**
  - YES, average CAC and profit is good but cash flow is slow → **Cash flow constraint**
  - NO, CAC is too high → **Check if it's leads (ads) or sales (conversion) that's broken**

## 4. SUPPLY-CONSTRAINED BUSINESS SECTION

### **Definition of Supply-Constrained:**
- You have demand but can't serve it
- Wish you could take on more customers but can't because of other issues (people, talent, capacity)
- Cannot handle the volume you have
- **Timestamp:** 1:31-1:37, 2:02:14-2:07:15

### **The Restaurant Analogy & Philosophy:**
While not explicitly stated as a traditional analogy, the concept is: You have customers waiting to buy, but your kitchen/team can't produce enough. Unlike demand-constrained (no customers coming in), you have the opposite problem.

### **Examples of Supply-Constrained Industries:**
**Timestamp:** 2:02:48-2:04:09

1. **High-volume, low-wage roles:**
   - Healthcare workers (therapists, social workers, care providers)
   - These roles are hard to find and scale

2. **Trades:**
   - HVAC, plumbing, electricians, welders, specialized craftspeople
   - Very common to have supply constraints
   - Machine shop example: Turned down $13M in projects due to lack of people

3. **High-end white-collar services:**
   - Consulting (like Hormozi's business)
   - Accounting, legal, investing
   - Very hard to find, very skilled people needed

4. **Service businesses with physical delivery:**
   - Home services, brick-and-mortar businesses
   - Businesses requiring people on trucks, routes
   - Subcontractors, technicians

### **Symptoms of Supply Constraints:**
**Timestamp:** 2:04:57-2:05:58

1. **Wait lists for services** - Can't onboard customers fast enough
2. **Personal overwork** - Working 70+ hours a week with no relief
3. **Turning away qualified customers** - Literally can't take on more jobs
4. **Quality declining due to volume** - Hiring bodies instead of quality people, causing churn
5. **Staff fully utilized but still can't keep up** - Even maximized people can't handle demand
6. **High employee churn** - Burning out talent by overworking them

### **Supply-Constrained Critical Insight:**
**Timestamp:** 2:06:21-2:06:44

"Since manpower means employees, it just means a different type of customer becomes the target. So if you are supply constrained, it means you're just demand constrained for employees."

**Translation:** A supply-constrained business is not actually different—you're just demand-constrained for a different customer (employees instead of end customers). The entire framework applies, but inverted.

### **LTGP to CAT Metric:**
**Definition:** Lifetime Gross Profit per employee ÷ Cost of Acquiring Talent

**Timestamp:** 2:06:49-2:07:15

**Key Quote:** "What does it cost me to get talent and what do I make on that talent?"

**Story (Insurance company mentor):**
- Stuck at $10M/year for years
- Changed recruiting method: Instead of hiring individual agents, ran contests for media creators
- Got 1,000 applicants, 50 completed work, picked 5
- Unlocked $50M+ business by improving recruiting efficiency (lowering CAT)

### **Selling Labor with a Markup:**
**Reference:** 2:25:28-2:26:30

The concept that some businesses are fundamentally buying labor (hiring people) and selling it with a markup. The entire constraint equation becomes:
- Can you afford the labor cost?
- Can you hire the talent?
- Can you pay enough to attract talent?
- Can you systemize the work enough to margin it?

Examples: Staffing agencies, home services, contracting, agencies selling labor-intensive services.

### **Solutions for Supply Constraints:**

**After solving lead/sales/LTV problems, supply constraint solutions:**

1. **Better hiring process (same as customer sales funnel):**
   - Job title as ad copy (e.g., "Customer Success" attracts better people than "Customer Support")
   - Page flow optimization (application process)
   - Lead nurture (follow up with applicants)
   - VSSL for job (video explaining role, culture, opportunity)
   - Better interview training
   - Better reputation/brand
   - Add friction to reduce junk applicants
   - Group interviews instead of 1-on-1 (mental health therapist example: 20 people at 60 seconds each, pick winners, follow up 1-on-1)

2. **Better avatars (target better talent):**
   - Change job title to attract caliber of person you need
   - Target people who want the role you're offering (not people desperate for any job)

3. **Money (increase what you pay):**
   - This is the most direct lever but requires cash flow
   - Solution stack: Improve sales → Raise prices → Get cash flow → Pay more
   - Example: HVAC company—install sales motion + pricing increase = unlock cash flow = pay more = hire more people = serve more capacity

4. **Productivity per person (get more out of each):**
   - Change delivery ratio (5 clients per rep → 10 clients per rep)
   - Done for you → Done with you
   - Cap usage (limit hours/effort per account)
   - Time-block management (manage when people work on what projects)
   - Often can 2x or 3x effectiveness just through time management

5. **Offshore/nearshore:**
   - Different labor pool at lower cost

6. **Strategic training program:**
   - Instead of hiring experienced people (hard to find), hire smart people fresh and train them
   - Strategy: Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, BCG, etc. all do this
   - Pay six figures to college grads, intensive training, but can't find experienced people at market rate
   - 6-month training gets them to 3-year experience level

7. **Raising prices + systemization:**
   - Example: Tax strategist—book solid for 6 months, raise prices, change delivery ratio, hire associates, backfill with better training/recruiting

### **Most Important Supply-Constrained Insight:**
**Timestamp:** 2:29:43-2:30:41

If you're in equilibrium (revenue steady, working hard, not growing), you're likely **demand-constrained**, not supply-constrained:
- You can probably do 30% more if you had to
- Solution: Break equilibrium with demand increase → Get cash flow → Expand supply
- Don't expand supply before you have demand, or you'll be underwater

**Rule:** Generate cash first, then expand capacity.

## 6. KEY CONCEPTS & VOCABULARY DEFINITIONS

### **Learning & Behavior**
- **Learning as Behavior Change:** Consuming content only counts as learning if it changes your behavior. Otherwise it's just entertainment.
- **Skills as Chained Adaptive Behaviors:** Individual skills are built from chains of smaller adaptive behaviors. Basketball isn't one skill; it's dribbling + shooting + passing, each with sub-skills.

### **Business Metrics & Formulas**
- **CAC (Cost to Acquire Customer):** What you spend to get one customer
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** Total profit you make from one customer over their lifetime
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** Ideal is 3:1 or better (make 3 for every 1 you spend)
- **LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit per employee):** Total profit generated by one employee over employment
- **CAT (Cost of Acquiring Talent):** What it costs to hire one employee
- **LTGP:CAT Ratio:** Equivalent to LTV:CAC but for hiring (how much you make per employee hired)

### **The "Black Box" Model**
- **Concept:** The business is a black box. You jam demand in the front (leads) and money comes out the back. The middle (operations, delivery) is the black box.
- **Timestamp:** 2:02:29-2:02:40
- **Implication:** Some constraints are on the front (leads/sales), some are in the middle (delivery/operations), some are output-related (money/profit/cash)

### **Constraint Types**
- **Demand-Constrained:** You have capacity but need more customers (most businesses start here)
- **Supply-Constrained:** You have demand but can't serve it (people/talent/capacity issue)
- **Both are demand problems:** Supply-constrained is just demand-constrained for employees

### **Pricing Frameworks**
- **Value-Based Pricing:** Price based on value delivered to customer, not cost
- **Cost-Plus Pricing:** Price = Cost + Margin (inferior approach)
- **Contextual Pricing:** Price based on context, avatar, perceived value (superior)
- **Price Increase Mechanics:**
  - Announce with 60-90 day notice (grandfathering old customers)
  - Use "price increase letter" template to frame it
  - Pair with improved delivery/sales for justification
  - Can often 2x or 3x prices without losing customers if paired with better processes

### **Sales & Conversion**
- **VSSL (Video Sales System Letter):** Pre-recorded video preloading sales pitch so calls focus on prospect instead of pitch
- **Lead Nurture:** Follow-up system to keep leads warm until ready to buy
- **Proof/Social Proof:** Testimonials, case studies, results, credentials embedded in sales process
- **Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO):** Improving % of visitors who take desired action (sign up, buy, call, etc.)

### **Offers & Money Models**
- **Giveaway Model:** Get result for customer free, charge for premium version
- **Win Your Money Back:** Customer gets money back if they don't get result
- **Decoy Offer:** Present premium option first, make original option look better by comparison
- **Pay Less Now, Pay More Later:** Monthly installments more expensive than annual prepay
- **Layaway Model:** Pay 50% upfront, we start work, finish paying while we deliver
- **BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later):** Third-party financing (typically increases sales 35%)

### **Content & Brand**
- **Hooks:** Opening lines/concepts that grab attention and communicate value
- **Branding:** Associations and pairings made in customer's mind (speed, quality, safety, etc.)
- **Ad Copy:** Headlines, subheadlines, value propositions that sell
- **Content Marketing:** Free, valuable content that builds authority and trust
- **Brand Loop:** When you do good work → people talk → reputation grows → lower CAC → better customers → do better work (compounding cycle)

### **Hiring & Talent**
- **Attitude vs. Aptitude:** Hire for smallest skill deficiency; in low-skill roles, hire attitude; in high-skill roles, hire aptitude
- **Avatar for Hiring:** The ideal candidate profile (title, experience, skill level)
- **Recruiting Funnel:** Same as sales funnel—job title as ad copy, application process as conversion, interviews as sales calls
- **Group Interviews:** Interview many candidates at once (60 seconds each) to increase throughput

### **Customer Satisfaction & Retention**
- **Churn:** Rate at which customers leave/cancel
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score):** How likely customers are to recommend (not explicitly defined but referenced)
- **Delivery Excellence:** Delivering on promise better than expected

### **Strategic Concepts**
- **Arbitrage:** Buying low, selling high (can apply to customers: cheap CAC, high LTV customer)
- **Market Saturation:** When you've reached max customers in a market, need new market or new avatar
- **Scaling:** Growing business while maintaining or improving unit economics
- **Leverage:** Using money/systems/people to get more return on effort
- **Compounding:** When improvements build on each other (reputation improves → cheaper CAC → better customers → reputation better)

## 7. AI/IMPLEMENTATION SECTION

### **ACQAI Tool Overview**
**Timestamp:** 3:50:30-4:16:45

Hormozi developed an AI tool (ACQAI/Alex AI) specifically designed for business consultation that:

1. **Follows the same logic path as human consultation:**
   - Not a generic ChatGPT wrapper
   - Built on the constraint framework and decision tree
   - Internal tool that Hormozi's team uses, now available to customers

2. **Key features:**
   - Context layer (personalized tab) to filter answers based on business type
   - Asks follow-up questions (like Hormozi would) instead of giving immediate answers
   - Pattern-matching ability to recognize business models and metrics
   - Integrated with subscription model recommendations

3. **How to use it:**
   - Input business details (offer type, customer acquisition method, revenue, employees, tech stack, current constraint)
   - For multiple businesses: Change the personalized context tab for each
   - Start new chat for each new question
   - AI will ask clarifying questions to refine diagnosis

4. **Example outputs from workshop:**
   - Kevin (investment coaching): AI diagnosed leads constraint after lost Instagram partnership, recommended content + DM funnel strategy
   - Ivan (talent recruitment): AI diagnosed need for better lead generation channels, recommended Facebook ads + improved qualifying
   - Insurance agent: AI identified LTV problem despite good CAC and conversion metrics, recommended avatar upgrade + vendor diversification

### **Why AI Tool is Superior:**
- **Contextual:** Not just answering question generically; filtered through specific business model
- **Question-First:** Asks questions before recommending (Socratic method)
- **Constraint-Aware:** Applies the framework systematically
- **Testable:** Can be validated against known business outcomes

## 9. SUPPLEMENTARY FRAMEWORKS & CONCEPTS

### **The Entrepreneurial Trap: The Cycle of Uninformed Optimism**
**Timestamp:** 2:47:35-2:49:04

Five stages entrepreneurs go through:
1. **Uninformed Optimism:** "I don't know what I don't know; this will be easy!"
2. **Informed Pessimism:** "Oh wow, this is harder than I thought."
3. **Valley of Despair:** "This is really, really hard. Maybe I should quit."
4. **Informed Optimism:** "I understand the challenges and I can solve them."
5. **Achievement:** "I did it."

**Problem:** Many entrepreneurs repeat stages 1-3 instead of pushing through because:
- Starting a new venture triggers stages 1-2 again (reward loop)
- Avoiding the valley of despair (stage 3) by jumping to new business
- Get reinforced for the bad behavior (jumping ship)

**Solution:** Realize the second business will have stage 3 too. Might as well push through current one and get to achievement.

### **Strategic Decision Framework: Hard Now vs. Hard Later**
**Timestamp:** 2:49:58-2:50:10

"What's the hard hard now versus how hard later? You're going to have to make the hard call and pick the baby that you want to keep."

When running multiple businesses/projects:
- Hard now: Kill the business/project that's not main focus
- Hard later: Burn out trying to manage both, both fail, lose years

Better to pick one and dominate than split focus.

### **Creative Outlet for Entrepreneurs with ADD**
**Timestamp:** 2:50:29-2:54:25

If you have entrepreneurial restlessness:
- Don't apply it to the business (you'll break things with too many changes)
- Apply it to marketing/content instead
- Hormozi's approach: Write books, create content, make lead magnets
- Gives outlet for innovation without destabilizing business operations
- Primary business functions best with stable operations, infrequent changes

### **Overexpansion = Undertalent**
**Timestamp:** 2:55:16-2:56:54

Opening second location before team is ready:
- One location: You cover everything personally
- Two locations: Performance drops at both
- Three locations: Even worse profit, even less coverage
- Pattern: More locations = more complexity = less ability to manage

**Root cause:** Undertalented team structure (not enough good decision-makers)

**Solutions:**
1. Kill yourself working to get above water (short term)
2. Give up profit for 6 months to hire strong manager
3. Sell/close location

**Principle:** Expand with talent, not before talent.

### **Compensation Dynamics**
**Timestamp:** 2:57:11-3:00:00

**Problem:** Promised compensation structure works when small but breaks at scale.

**Solution (Compensation Restructuring):**
1. Announce change 60-90 days out (not overnight)
2. Frame with business context (need growth to execute original vision)
3. Give 3-month runway to think it over
4. Have 3-month backfill period to replace if needed
5. Most people stay (understand it's market reality)

**On Overcompensation:**
- If you can't afford the best talent, you're not optimized
- Top 10-15 percentile talent in key lever-point roles = best ROI
- Example: Zuckerberg pays $100M signing bonuses for AI talent because ROI is there

**On Underpricing:**
- ~40% of service businesses are severely underpriced
- Price indicates quality (if you charge $1, you'll get demand for $1-quality work)
- Should continually raise prices as you improve, until you hit equilibrium with demand
- If you can do 100% on a $1 offer, you're too cheap

## 10. FINAL STRATEGIC PRINCIPLES

**From closing remarks (4:21:02-4:24:33):**

### **Strategy is Resource Allocation**
"Strategy is dictated by how you choose to allocate resources. Your time, your money, your effort are the raw resources that you have. Putting them towards the thing that is going to get you the highest return."

### **The Universal Constraint Diagnosis Process**

1. **Can I do more?**
   - If no: Check metrics, market, model, mindset
   - If yes: Go to next question

2. **Do I have my numbers?**
   - If no: Prioritize tracking, pause growth
   - If yes: Next question

3. **Is the market big enough?**
   - If no: Change market or model
   - If yes: Next question

4. **Will this model get me to my goal?**
   - If no: Change model
   - If yes: Next question

5. **Am I all in?**
   - If no: Get all in or change plan
   - If yes: Identify money constraint

6. **Which money constraint? (Pick one)**
   - Leads too expensive? → Better marketing, ads, content, offer, avatar
   - Can't convert? → Better sales process, VSSL, proof, training
   - Don't make enough per customer? → Raise prices, increase LTV, better avatar
   - Cash flow too slow? → Accelerate payment terms, prepay incentives, AR financing, payment restructuring

7. **Have you solved that?**
   - If no: Keep working on it
   - If yes: Repeat entire process for manpower/supply constraints

### **On Building for Compounding**

Goal: Build business where each customer/hire/action increases future value.

Methods:
- **Reputation:** Good work → referrals → lower CAC
- **Brand:** Trust built → higher prices
- **Network effects:** Each user makes network more valuable
- **Systems:** Better processes → better outcomes → better brand
- **Retention:** Long customer relationships → LTV increases

Must have "equal and opposing force" (brand/reputation) to offset increased CAC as you scale to colder markets.

### **Core Thesis**

The workshop is designed to accomplish three things:

1. **Behavior #1:** Maximize leverage by allocating attention to highest-return activities
2. **Behavior #2:** Select correct playbook and execute (eliminate guessing)
3. **Behavior #3:** Increase conviction via proof (AI tool, live examples)

If you do these three things, you will scale your business.
