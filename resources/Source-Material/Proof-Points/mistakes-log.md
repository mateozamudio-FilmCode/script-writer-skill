# Mistakes Log — Slow on Asset Builds & Poor Future Prediction

**Date:** February 15, 2025  
**Category:** Mistakes Log  
**Severity:** Recurring Pattern

---

## The Mistake

I move too slowly on asset builds and don't predict the future well enough.

---

## Where This Showed Up

- **CRO Hire:** Didn't build the role architecture documentation (the asset) before hiring. Moved fast on the person, slow on the system. Six months and a full salary lost because the infrastructure wasn't built before the hire walked in the door.
- **Institutional Knowledge:** Never built an Exit Extraction Protocol as a standing asset. When the revenue manager left, tribal knowledge walked out with them. The asset (documented SOPs, playbooks, processes) should have been built in real time — not recovered after departure.
- **Content Infrastructure:** Had the stories, frameworks, and proof points sitting in lived experience for months before starting to systematize them into a content bible. The asset was there — just undocumented and therefore undeployable.

---

## The Pattern

The failure isn't speed of decision-making — it's speed of **asset creation**. I make moves (hiring, scaling, pivoting) before the supporting infrastructure exists to make those moves sustainable. The prediction gap compounds this: I don't forecast what I'll need 90 days from now and build it today.

---

## The Fix

- **Pre-build assets before triggers.** If a hire is coming, the Role Architecture Doc exists first. If content is launching, the content bible is complete first. If a system is scaling, the SOPs are documented first.
- **Run a 90-day asset forecast.** Every month, ask: "What assets will I desperately need in 90 days that I'm not building right now?" Build them before the urgency hits.
- **Use AI to accelerate asset builds.** Claude and automation tools close the gap between "I need this" and "this exists as a documented, usable system." Stop treating AI as a recovery tool and start treating it as a pre-build tool.

---

## Key Lesson

Moving fast on decisions without moving fast on assets is just creating future fires. The goal isn't slower decisions — it's faster infrastructure.
